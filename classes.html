Vinyasa Yoga

Vinyasa is a vibrant, active, and temperature increase form of yoga in which we flow from one yoga practice to the next while linking every move with the exhale. This practice helps clear, move, and flex the entire body, but it also works to gain strength. Vinyasa flow is not suggested for beginners unless they are prepared to keep their bodies safe and not overload them if they experience physical discomfort.

Yoga for beginners

This is a hatha yoga practice that aims to introduce the basic principles of yoga to the practitioner. Getting into the poses remains within the comforting sense of effort. It emphasizes the synchronization between breathing and movement. Active poses are balanced with passive, relaxing poses to achieve a more profound understanding of the effect of any dynamic posture on the body.

Ashtanga yoga

Ashtanga yoga is a hatha yoga style that includes a strictly defined sequence of asanas that are synchronized with particular ujjayi breathing and gaze concentration. This is a joint-opening strengthening exercise.

Kundalini Yoga

Kundalini Yoga is a dynamic practice that incorporates sharp excretions, static positions, rhythm, deep breathing, sound, start dancing, relaxation, and meditation. The practice level is moderate.

Meditation

Meditation is a procedure and practice that should be done in a good seating position. It may include gentle spinal movement as well as deep breathing. The physiological load on the class is minimal.
